#雅思# 2020年1-4月口语答案sample answers--part 1

2020-01-30 / 93 min read

哈喽，2020年1月的结尾，但是我真的希望可以重启一下，这个年的开始，不是很尽人意。最近我的心情有些低落，但是想造福一下或许同在焦虑心情下的小烤鸭们，所以免费发放一些这个季度的雅思口语答案。需要告诉大家的是，一. 这个答案不是我--这个单打独斗的普通英语学习者写的（非原创）；二. 既然这个答案在市面上流通的这么广，那么肯定还有很多人会有，为了避免重复的风险，建议参考这个答案小伙伴们一定要适当调整一下说法，不要无脑地死背。同时附上音频，希望可以帮助到大家的语音练习。

如有需要单个题音频的小伙伴，可以留言。

常考题

Work or Study

  1. Why did you choose that subject?
    I am majoring in International Business. I chose my major subject of study based on my aptitude in studying economics, accounting, and languages, my interest in travel, and advice from my parents and teachers. I felt this major area of study would prepare me for a career that I would find interesting and that I could be successful in.

I chose business as a subject because it gives me an excellent foundation to enter my desired career upon completion of my studies. It is important for me to take the same career path as my father, as he has had an incredibly successful one, and I would like to emulate that.

  1. Is it interesting?
    I truly enjoy my courses because they are so interesting. There are a few courses that are more difficult for me than others, but I find if I put more time and effort into studying, I am able do well in them.

Yes, it brings lots of fun. I get quite some time to personally interact with my teachers, enabling me to go into great depth while studying material that I find particularly interesting. In addition, by sharing impressive knowledge on the subject in my online blog, I have attracted thousands of fans. I really love.

  1. What subjects are you studying?
    This year, I’m taking advanced classes in Finance, Macroeconomics, English, and Information Technology. I am also taking a class in Modern European History because I thought this would be interesting.

I am currently studying both business marketing and psychology as a joint degree. These two courses give me both a theoretical business knowledge on marketing campaigns and practical working skills. It also provides me with a psychological background that allows me to understand the reasons behind various aspects of those topics better.

  1. Do you prefer to study in the mornings or in the afternoon?
    I prefer to study in the afternoon. I feel more alert in the afternoon than I do in the morning or the evening. I seem to be able to read, and comprehend more, in less time.

Compared with many friends who like to study in the afternoons, I prefer to study in the mornings With a g-hour sleep at night, I am very energetic in the mornings, and I can better focus on solving severe problems.

  1. Are you looking forward to working?
    I am definitely looking forward to using the things I have learned in school in my career. I’m also looking forward to making money to support myself.

Yes, I am. Even though working can be stressful and challenging, I do enjoy it. However, finishing my studies and starting my career is exciting, as I will have the opportunity to apply what I learned. Besides, with a salary, I can be financially independent, and buy anything I like without worry about my parents rejections.

  1. Do you like your subject?
    I love my subject because it is so interesting. I find the more I learn, the more I find interesting about it! I think everything I am learning will be useful in my career.

I do, because it directly relates to my desired career path. The subject offers a perspective on the marketing business and interesting knowledge about human psychology. The combination of these two aspects is going to help me open an advertisement business.

  1. What work do you do?
    I recently started as a research assistant at a large international advertising firm. It’s an entry level position where I find statistics about the likes and dislikes of different age groups in a specific country that our client is looking to do business in.

I currently work as a marketing assistant at a large company that sells electronic products. In particular, I design advertising posters for the global launch of new products in the Chinese market.

  1. Why did you choose to do that type of work?
    While this is not the final job that I was hoping for, it is a solid entry-level position in a firm that does the kind of work I am interested in. I enjoy gathering the statistics knowing that they will be used to make important decisions in marketing our client’s product. I feel there is a lot of room for promotion here in this company as well.

I chose this work to follow my fathers footsteps. My father has been working in marketing for his entire life, Since I was very young, my father shared several insights about his work in the form of curious stories. These stories always caught my interest, which later transformed into my passion.

  1. Do you miss being a student?
    I didn’t think that I would, but I do.As much as I like my job and I am grateful to have it, it is a big change. I am meeting new people and learning new responsibilities; my daily schedule is very different, but I need to expect a period of adjustment.

Yes and no. I miss the time spent studying with my friends and working towards a standard qualification. However, since finishing my studies, I do prefer the freedom of being able to move on and start my career.

  1. Do you like your job?
    I really enjoy my job. I think the work is interesting and look forward to learning more. I like all of the people I work with. There are two other research assistants who were hired the same time I was and I enjoy working with them.

Yes, I do. My job allows me to interact with a variety of people from different backgrounds and cultures every day. I find the work itself to be extremely fulfilling, and I really appreciate the way my efforts get rewarded.

Home

  1. Can you describe the place where you live?
    I live in a high rise apartment building on the 5th floor overlooking the street with apartment buildings on either side. We have a balcony where we keep many of our plants and sometimes we’ll sit there in the evening.

  2. How long have you live there?
    I have lived there for 18 years. I was born there.

  3. Who do you live with?
    I live with my parents, my sister, and my dog. Sometimes my grandparents or aunt will visit us and they stay with us in our home.

  4. Do you plan to live there for a long time?
    I’m hoping after I finish my education, I find a job and I can find my own home. Housing is expensive, so I may be home with my parents longer than I plan to be.

  5. What is the difference between where you are living now and where you have lived in the past?
    I haven’t lived anywhere else. I have always lived here. My parents lived in a smaller apartment in a building nearby before I was born, but they moved here because they needed more room.

  6. Do you prefer living in a house or a flat?
    I prefer living in a flat because it is so much more convenient, and neighbours seem much more friendly. Perhaps I like it because this is what I am accustomed to.

  7. In the future, what type of place would you like to live in?
    I would like to live in a flat on the very top floor of a building. I think it would be quieter and we wouldn’t be able hear as much street noise and the view, especially at night, would be spectacular.

  8. What kind of neighbourhood /environment/surroundings/home would you like to live in?
    I like living in the city where I am. I like the convenience of being very close to public transportation so I can go where ever I need to go. I like my neighbourhood because we have a variety of young people and old people. It’s our own community.

  9. What do you usually do in your house/flat/room?
    We eat, sleep, and basically live together there. When we are all at home, we sometimes do things together, like watching sports on TV, other times we will go to our room to study or listen to music.

  10. Which room does your family spend most of the time in?
    We spend most of our time in the living room. We will watch TV there or play games while lying on the floor. It’s the sunniest room in the house, so it is very pleasant to be in.

  11. What part of your home do you like the most?
    I like my bedroom the most in my home. I have it decorated to my taste and feel safe and calm whenever I go into it.

  12. Are the transport facilities to your home very good?
    Absolutely, that’s one of the reasons I like our neighbourhood. It only takes a few minutes to walk to the bus stop and from there, I can get anywhere in the city.

Hometown

1.What’s (the name of) your hometown?
I live in Guangzhou. It is located in the Guangdong Province. and is known as the City of Flowers.

  1. Is that a big city or a small place?
    It is the third largest city in China. It has a population of over 13 million people. It’s a big place.

  2. Please describe your hometown a little.
    It is the educational and cultural centre of Southern China. It’s also known as the City of Flowers because there are evergreens and flowers in bloom year round because of its climate. It’s a pretty city.

  3. How long have you lived there?
    I have lived here all of my life, for eighteen years. My parents have lived here all of their lives too.

  4. Do you like it?
    I like it very much. Of course, this is all I have ever known, but whenever my family travels anywhere, I am always happiest when I return home.

  5. Do you like living there?
    I like living here because my family is here, my grandparents and aunt live here too. I like the climate because it never gets really cold. I don’t really like all of the rain we get in the summer, but even then, the rain keeps the air cleaner.

  6. What do you like (the most) about your hometown?
    I really like the food. The Cantonese food is excellent and there are so many other kinds of international restaurants to try. I also really enjoy Thai food.

  7. Is there anything you dislike about it?
    The humidity gets things damp in the house, so we have to use air conditioning a lot of the time. I don’t really like air conditioning, but we don’t have a choice but to use it.

  8. Do you think you will continue living there for a long time? / 10. Do you plan to continue living there? ( Combined two similar questions as one)
    I want to go abroad to study after I graduate from high school. I want to see what other cities and countries have to offer and then I’ll decide.

The area you live in

  1. Do you like the area that you live in?
    I love the area that I live in. I am so lucky that we have so much to do which are very close to us! Also, there is public transportation just one block from my building. Everything is so convenient!

  2. What are some changes in the area recently?
    Over the past few years, some new restaurants have opened nearby which make eating out more convenient for everyone in the area. Also, last year, improvements were made to the park near my house. Some older trees were trimmed and some new varieties of trees were planted.

  3. Do you know any famous people in your area?
    Well, I really don’t think any famous people live near me. I have never heard of or met any famous person in my neighbourhood. I guess it’s because the area I am living in is an area for middle income families, not for famous people who have much higher salaries.

  4. Where do you like to go in that area?
    Like I said earlier, there are some new restaurants in the area. They are family-style restaurants, so they are the great places to go with my family for relaxed meals. Also, the park has always been a pleasant place to go. Everything I need or want is within walking distance to my home.

老题

Music

  1. When do you listen to music?
    I listen to music whenever I can. I enjoy listening to music when I'm on public transportation, going to and from school. I also like to listen to music sometimes when I'm studying. It helps to
    block out things in the background so I'm able to focus better.

I listen to music whenever I have an opportunity to do so. This is mainly when I am walking to work in the morning. I love music a lot, as it makes me relaxed and happy.

2.. How much time do you spend listening to music every day?
I haven't really thought about that. I think I spend about 2 to 3 hours a day. I listen to it on public transportation and then when I study, but not all the time when I study. Sometimes it's distracting when I'm studying, but I would say 2 to 3 hours a day is a fair estimate.

Putting an exact timescale on it is hard. On an average day I would estimate that I listen to music for at least 3 or 4 hours On weekends, I spend even more time, as I like to have relaxing background music throughout the day.

3.. What kinds of music do you like to listen to?
I really enjoy K-pop, the energy and the lyrics, it’s just so lively and makes me feel good. I also like to listen to slower songs that have a deeper story like a ballad or something- a lot of fun to listen to a ballad. I also like to listen to instrumental music where there are no words, I sometimes find that to be very soothing when I'm trying to study.

I have a very eclectic music taste. Depending on what mood I am in, I can listen to anything from blues and reggae, to rock and metal music. Lately, I have been listening to Pink Floyds quite a lot.

  1. What’s your favourite kind of music?
    Without question my favourite music is K-pop. You can't listen to it without enjoying it. It's lively and it makes you feel good and when you're listening to it you're tapping your foot and just getting ready to be excited for the rest of your day. It just always puts me in a good mood.

My favourite kind of music is rock-and-roll. In particular, I like the music by Pink Floyd and Black Sabbath, but I also love newer bands like Avenged Sevenfold. Despite my love for this kind of music, sometimes, I listen to the top pop songs of the moment.

  1. Have you ever been to a concert before?
    Sadly, I have not been to a concert, but I'd like to go to one soon. Concerts are expensive but I'm saving up money and I have a few concerts that I have that in mind that I'd like to go to.

I have been to many concerts in different countries, including the UK and Singapore. I love the feeling I have at a concert where live music is more touching, I was at a concert quite recently, when I went to see Guns and Roses perform in Lisbon.

Tea and coffee

  1. Do Chinese people like to drink tea or coffee?
    I think more Chinese people prefer to drink tea than coffee. I know my family does. There are so many more flavours of tea that people can enjoy, and that’s why it is more popular.

I think most Chinese people drink both tea and coffee. However, coffee is more common to drink early in the morning, while tea is something you can drink all day long. Tea is a bit of a comforting drink, as well.

  1. Do you prepare tea or coffee for the guests at home?
    I prepare and serve tea for guests at home. My mother will make coffee for the few guests who prefer coffee, because I don’t know how to make it properly.

Whenever I have people pay me a visit, I usually make them a cup of tea. This is especially true if they stop by for dinner or simply in the afternoon.

  1. When was the last time you drank tea or coffee?
    I drank some green tea this morning. I have tea every morning. When I get home from school, I will often have an herbal tea. I have only tried coffee once, and frankly, I just don’t like it. It tastes very bitter to me.

I have to confess, I' ve had tea and coffee today. This morning, I had a latte for breakfast. I also washed my lunch down with a cup of green tea. They were both delicious.

Photos

  1. Do you like to take photos?
    In truth, I am not the sort of person who would go around snapping photos or selfies. I only do so when I want to capture a special moment, and I would use my phones camera instead of a professional one Good for me that taking photos these days is as easy as pie, otherwise I would end up without a single picture.

  2. Do you prefer to take photos yourself or to have other people take photos?
    I usually have other people take photos for me. I somehow trust they would render better pictures than me. Photography has never been my strength, which is why I would rather let others do it. Then I get them to send me the photos which I keep on my phone and only rarely develop at the photo studio.

  3. How often do you take photos?
    I don't take photos quite often. There must be a special event or moment to prompt me to snap a picture. It could be something funny, or even a beautiful view out in nature. All in all, I can say I take photos once in a blue moon. It's just not my cup of tea.

  4. In what situations do you take photos?
    Special events such as parties, celebrations and gatherings are worth remembering so I take photos during these. In addition, I might also photograph interesting places if I am on a trip abroad. However, I avoid taking pictures that reveal my daily routine. I think such photos don't have any added value, plus I believe they are too personal to share with others.

  5. How do you keep your photos?
    I store most of my photos on my smartphone. I have an empty album at home but I have yet to develop some photos and fill it in. I prefer to store the pictures electronically because they cannot be mutilated. Plus, I have the photos with me at all times so I can show them to others.

Patience

  1. Were you patient when you were young?
    No, I was not patient at all when I was young. I remember having trouble waiting for meals and interrupting my parents for attention when they were busy. I cried and shouted when I was impatient and could not be quiet at all.

As a kid, I was the opposite of patient. It was as tough as nails for me to sit still and wait for anything had to put a lot of work into this aspect of my life.

  1. How do you feel when other people are not patient?
    When younger children are impatient, it doesn’t bother me because I understand they are still learning. However, I get upset when older children and adults aren’t patient. It’s important to be respectful of other people.

When an adult isn‘t patient, I find it very frustrating. Impatience only makes a situation more complicated. However, I can't help but empathise with children that have ants in their pants, because they have so much energy.

  1. Were you less or more patience when you are angry?
    I am more patient when I am angry. This is because I always walk away until I am calmer. Then, I won’t do or say things that I may regret later. Walking away forces me to be calmer, so then I will be more patient.

I'li admit, when I'm angry, my patience often goes out the door. I try to keep my cool, but if I am pushed too far, then I find it hard to deal with situations that require patience.

Public holiday

  1. How many public holidays do you have in China?
    There are seven public holidays we can enjoy in China. If it’s counted by days, there are 11 public holidays each year. For some of the Chinese, they can enjoy one or half a day for their own holiday. For example, Chinese women enjoy half a day on Women’s Day.

We have seven public holidays and China. These holidays include the Spring Festival, National Day and New Year, just to name a few. Of course, each holiday is important to our country, because it celebrates our culture.

  1. Do you think people need more public holidays?
    In my opinion, I think people need more public holidays in China. With more and more work and study burden, Chinese people have less time to enjoy their own hobbies or to attend their family activities nowadays. In addition, more holidays can encourage people to travel more.

I think we could use more holidays. I love to celebrate with my family, friends and community while enjoying the spirit of a special occasion. Holidays give people the chance to kick back and relax too, which is important.

  1. How do you usually spend your holidays?
    Usually I spend my holidays with my family or my friends. I often gather with family at a restaurant for a morning tea or a meal. Sometimes I will go to the cinema with my good friends for a movie. If I have more holidays, I would rather travel with my family or friends.

Generally, when there is a holiday, my parents and I go to my grandparents' home. Then, we eat a ton of food, play games and enjoy a community event. This often includes setting off and watching fireworks.

  1. Which holiday is your favourite?
    My favourite holiday, without a doubt, is the Spring Festival which is our Chinese New Year. This is a traditional Chinese festival and it’s the most important holidays for Chinese families. Normally, no matter where we are, we go home to meet our parents and grandparents during these days in a year.

My favourite holiday is the Spring Festival. There is something refreshing about greeting a new year. Plus, I love spending time with family, passing out red envelopes and eating delicious foods.

Outdoor

  1. Do you prefer to be indoors or outdoors?
    Personally, I prefer being outdoors rather than indoors. It feels great to go outside and exercise a bit to maintain a healthy lifestyle. I go to the gym once a week to keep fit and get slimmer. I also do yoga at home for two hours after dinner.

I have developed a habit of relaxing indoors, especially on workdays. However, when I am on vacation, the great outdoors is my personal oasis. I can't get enough of the sunshine and scenic views.

  1. Did you like to go outside when you were young?
    I loved to go outside when I was young. There was a park nearby my house when I was in primary school, and my parents would often bring me over there to have fun with my friends. We could spend the whole day in the park without getting bored.

When I was a kid, I was often tempted to stay indoors and play video games. Once I worked up the motivation to go outside, I had a blast, though. I always felt much happier after spending the day outdoors.

  1. Did you often go over to your friends’ house when you were young?
    Yes, I did. My friends invite me to their houses quite a lot and I’ve developed a good relationship with their parents. They often treat me with food and drinks and chat with me about interesting things about their work. The things I learned from them were fascinating.

As soon as I was old enough to venture out on my own, I spent a lot of time with my friends. We would take turns going to each others houses, which helped to keep things fresh and exciting.

  1. Is it important for children to play outdoors?
    I think it’s really important for people to encourage their children to play outdoors. Having some outdoor activities could keep children healthy and strong. Playing outdoors would also help them release some stress that was built during long time studies and school work.

Absolutely! Playing outside is a great way for children to get some exercise, learn and use their imagination. Apart from that, fresh air and sunshine are both vital to our health and the best place to get both is outside.

Plan

  1. Do you make plans every day?
    Not necessarily. I make plans for myself about things that I must complete each day, and I would mark off any tasks that I’ve completed after doing it. However, I don’t really specify what time I would complete it because it doesn’t really matter to me as long as I complete it in the specified date.

I actually tend to wing it most of the time. I would love to be a planner, but usually, things don't go according to plan, which frustrates me greatly. So, I just let things flow naturally.

  1. Are you good at managing your time?
    I’m pretty good at managing my time. I often estimate the amount of time that’s required to finish a certain task, and save some time between each task as little breaks.

I have to admit; time management isn't my cup of tea. I tend to space out often and lose track of time. I also work better when I procrastinate, as it forces me to concentrate harder.

  1. What is the latest plan you’ve made?
    Just a few days ago, a childhood friend of mine that I haven’t seen for a long time contacted me on social media， asking me to go to a reunion party and see my old primary school friends at a restaurant. It has been quite some time since we parted ways after graduating primary school, so I made plans with them and wrote it in my agenda.

The last plan I made was to go home to visit my grandparents. I hadn't seen them in a little while, so I cleared my schedule and planned out the best travel route to maximise my time with them.

  1. What is the hardest part about making plans?
    The hardest part about making plans for me is when something unexpected occurs and disrupts my original plan. I get stood up by some friends sometimes, because they only tell me an hour before the meetups. It’s really hard to adapt to the sudden change in plan, but I manage to work them out in the end.

I think the hardest aspect of planning is working out the kinks of each plan. You never know what is going to go wrong, so you really have to plan around a bunch of different factors, including the weather.

Math

  1. Do you often use a calculator?
    Yes. I do often use a calculator because I feel that it is much faster and convenient when I am doing my Math exercises. Especially when I am solving some complicated algebra questions, a functional calculator can be used as a useful tool for it.

Ever since I figured out there is a calculator on my smartphone, I tend to use it frequently. It especially comes in handy when I'm shopping, as it allows me to calculate my purchases before checking out.

  1. Are girls generally good at mathematics?
    General speaking, girls are not as good as boys at mathematics, especially in the past. But nowadays, there are more and more girls who are interested in the field of Science and Engineering. Some of them are better than boys in the Math study and even at their work.

I feel that girls are just as good at math as boys are. However, for years, there was a stigma about mathematics and girls. So, many girls didn't put in the effort needed to excel in the subject.

  1. Do you think it is difficult to learn mathematics well?
    Yes. To learn mathematics well requires at least three main factors: interest, diligence, and some degree of abilities in abstract thinking and understanding. This is because compared with other subjects, Math involves more abstract concepts and logical inferences.

Mathematics can be a challenging subject. However, I feel that a person's motivation as well as the teachers they have throughout their education plays an important role in how they view the subject.

  1. Do you think mathematics is important?
    Mathematics is an important basic course for students to study because it educates and trains students for their comprehensive ability in abstract thinking, analysis and deduction. It is also the basic tool of the study in all science and engineering. And it plays an important role in the development of human society and propels the society forward.

Absolutely. Mathematics not only helps people to understand so much about the world, but it is also a necessity for the future. Without this subject, we would not have the technology or knowledge that we do today.

Travel

    1. Do you like travelling?
      Yes, I really enjoy travelling, especially by myself. It’s a great way to release all the stress built up at school. I go on some trips to different cities nearby every year during the summer break. I book and order all the tickets and flights by myself, and it’s more affordable.

Yes, I love traveling. I do enjoy seeing the world from great historical monuments to white sandy beaches. Travelling is a very interesting way to learn about new cultures and explore new places.

  1. Which cities have you travelled to?
    I’ve been to places like Nanjing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Beijing. I enjoyed all the sceneries and food during my trip. I’m still a student, I can’t afford to go to other countries and travel by myself. But I’ve been to Kyoto, Japan with my parents. It was a great family trip and we had a lot of fun there together.

I have traveled to many different parts of China recently and I enjoyed the trips a lot. To be more specific, my last visit was to Shenzhen, which is called Silicon Valley of China.

  1. What kind of cities do you like to travel to?
    I like going to the cities that I could try a lot of delicious food. I really enjoy trying out different food from different cities and experiencing the diffident cultures from places. You could also learn about the histories of places as you have the food. A lot of food such as stinky tofu can date back to Ancient China.

Actually, I am a big fan of history, so I love to visit historical cities in China, such as Beijing and Xian. Traveling to those cities enables me to explore the great history and traditional culture.

  1. What is the place that left you the deepest impression when travelling?
    Japan is probably the most impressive country I’ve ever been to. The clean streets and the politeness of the people have left me the deepest impressions. There was absolutely no garbage that was left on the ground, and all the places I’ve been to were extremely clean and tidy. People were also very polite when they interact with each other.

Well, Xian is an amazing city impressing me a lot. It has a history of over 6000 years and had been the capital of countries for 1200 years. There are lots of ancient attractions, such as the Terracotta Warriors, big wild goose pagoda and Famen Temple.

Market

  1. What do street markets sell?
    Out of all the street markets I’ve been to, almost all of them sell authentic Chinese street food, such as stinky tofu in Hunan, Changfen in Guangdong and other foods that are special in each continent. Street food are usually made to order and offered in smaller sizes for your convenience.

I've seen just about everything sold at a street market. You can find produce, meats, snacks, clothes, handicrafts and electronics. These little markets are truly remarkable, and they help keep the culture alive.

  1. Are there many street markets in China?
    There are quite a lot of street markets in China. If you go out on the streets in the middle of the night, you could see hundreds of people still sitting on the side of the streets, savouring the snacks they’ve bought from the vendors. It’s an unspoken tradition for people to have midnight snacks in China.

There are street markets everywhere in China. In the countryside, farmers sell their produce, meats and baked goods. In cities, you can find these things and so much more. There are even a few markets that are world-famous.

  1. What are the differences between street markets and supermarkets?
    Supermarkets tend to sell food that’s made using machines, such as frozen foods and boxed snacks. However, if you go to a local street market in China, you could find tons of homemade and authentic Chinese street food sold in different vendors. They are also cheaper than the frozen food sold in the supermarkets.

Supermarkets tend to be a large establishment that is filled with groceries, toiletries and more. Street markets are private vendors that sell items that they grow, make or sell in their own store.

  1. Do you often go to the supermarkets?
    I do. Even though supermarkets have a lot of frozen foods in the freezers, they still sell fresh produces. Living alone as a student, I go to the supermarkets once every week to stock up fresh produces in my fridge. I make most of my meals every week, other than the times where I’m incredibly tired after school

Whenever I need to get a lot of items, I tend to go to the supermarket. There, I can buy easy to make meals, as well as the toiletries and groceries that I need to make life convenient.

    No, I don’t. Compared to art, I like music more because music has a power of healing. Whenever I feel tired or anxious, I like to listen to some relaxing music.

I am a diehard fan of art. When talking about art, it is just natural to think of those big names like Van Gogh, Da Vinci, or something like that. But I think art can be cute and simple as well. Pop art with striking colours and round shapes is my cup of tea.

  1. Have you ever had art classes?
    Yes, I signed up for calligraphy class and painting class when I was in primary school. Every summer holiday, I enjoyed drawing natural scenery, big buildings and strip-cartoons with my crayon. My teacher often said I was talented.

Cake

  1. Do you like eating cakes?
    Yes, of course I love eating cakes. Doesn’t everyone? My favourite is mixed fruit cake. But I don’t like to eat those cakes with lots of cream. I like the fruit and chocolate cakes more.

Well, it depends. A super delicious cake requires quality cream, soft bread and superb cake-making skills. And a cake like that is dear in most cases. In contrast, a more affordable cake tastes like dust and sometimes even feet. So I like eating cakes when they are expensive.

  1. Have you ever made cakes?
    No, I haven’t. I am not that kind of person who is good at baking things. So, I haven’t tried to make cakes and I don’t think I will make cakes in the future.

I am a really bad cook whose most famous dishes are instant noodles and burnt toasts. So as you may probably guess, I have never ever made cakes of any kinds. But luckily, all my friends are interested in making desserts and cakes. You can often find them wherever an oven is equipped.

  1. What desserts do Chinese people like?
    I think many people like to eat sweets because they are cheap and easy to buy. Sweets come in many flavours and can satisfy many people's tastes.

At the end of most Chinese meals, seasonal fresh fruit is often served as dessert. But Chinese cuisine also has a number of popular sweets, some of which are only made for special holiday period. Take for example Chinese almond cookies, nian gao (a glutinous brown sugar cake), eight treasures rice pudding, etc.

  1. Do you like dessert?
    Yes. My favourite food is mixed fruit cake and chocolate cake. Although they are a high sugar and fatty food, I still can't help craving dessert every day.

I had a sweet tooth when I was a little girl. I was fond of whatever tastes sweet and contains a lot of sugar such as red velvet cakes, strawberry ice cream, marshmallows, lollipops and popsicles. But as come of age, I like dessert the least because my skin is sensitive to sugar and a bite of sweet food may well lead to a big red acne on my face. Thats disturbing. So I just reject all desserts.

History

  1. Did you like history when you were young?
    I liked history, but I disliked the history class. I think history is the most boring subject that I have ever taken. There is no skill to learn it. All that you need to do is to remember, remember and remember.

My answer is yes. I guess it is all because my father is a voracious reader of history. When I was alone at home, I used to pull out a thick book from my fathers tall and large bookshelf and thumb through the pages with illustrations and cartoons. In most cases, the book in my hands was a history book.

  1. When was the last time you read about history?
    I follow the topic of history in my Daily News App, so it will push me to read many interesting historical stories every day. It is very good App and I enjoy the history stories in it. The stories are brief, but I can get more information if I am interested.

Since I am an English major, I read British history voraciously back in college. **It all started by accident when I binge-watched**the popular British TV series Downton Abbey. The exquisite clothes, etiquette and decoration shown in the TV series captured my full attention. So I read about the making of the Great Britain.

  1. Have you ever been to historical museum?
    Yes, I have been to many historical museums. The biggest one that I have been to is the Forbidden City. But I think the most interesting historical museum is the Xi’An Historical Museum because Xi 'An has a long history and many stories took place in Xi 'An. Many important people were born and lived there, as well.

I rarely go to museums of any type, but I visited a lot of historical museums when I was an exchange student in the United Kingdom. To my surprise, there are historical museums in every city in the UK that are open to the public without charge. I learned many historical facts about the Great Britain thanks to these museums.

  1. Do you like history?
    Definitely. History was my favorite subject in senior high school. Unlike science which is all about calculus and formula, history tells us all kinds of interesting, exciting or tragic stories of humans past. I am all crazy about tales.

Science class

  1. Do you think science classes are important?
    I think they are very important, especially for children. Science class can arouse students' curiosity and cultivate their ability to explore the unknown.

I think science plays an indispensable role in our society. It is now the driving force in all aspects of our life, politics, culture and education. Without science, it may have taken longer for us to go back to our hometowns since high-speed railway would have not been possible.

  1. Did you have science class in primary school or high school?
    Yes, I did have science classes in primary school. Science class was always full of fun because the teacher would tell us many interesting facts about the natural world. She always presented the information in an engaging way.

I began to learn science in high school, namely biology, chemistry and physics. I followed what my science teachers taught in junior high school. And thanks to that, I often came on top in all the examinations. But senior high school was my nightmare, for the lessons taught in science classes were unbelievably hard to comprehend.

  1. Do you like science class?
    Yes, I like it very much. Science class is always interesting because I enjoy thinking about science and nature. I think my primary school teacher encouraged me to want to learn more.

Not at all. I hated science classes more than any subject, especially physics. Thats all Greek to me. Maybe I have just conformed to the notorious stereotype that girls are good at arts whereas boys learn science faster. I never get full marks in any science class.

  1. What kind of science did you do at school?
    Actually, I don’t remember exactly what I have learned in science classes in primary school, but I remember they were fun. In high school, I particularly enjoyed my science class in Biology.

The science classes were more interesting and interactive when I was in junior high Students could do science experiments in biology, physics and chemistry. But in senior high school, it was always exercises, study groups and papers.

Break

  1. How often do you take a break?
    I usually take a ten-minute break once every hour from whatever work I am doing. It is very essential for me so I can unwind and refocus on my task again.

I take a break every month, because China has traditional festivals/holidays every single month like Mid-autumn Festival, Dragon Boat Festival, etc. In most cases, it's short but refreshing.

  1. Why do you need to take a break?
    I think people need to take a break to relax so that they can recharge and focus on their task at hand again. Otherwise, they may feel tired and run out of energy. In a way, taking breaks enhance people’s working efficiency.

As the saying goes, all work and no play make Jack a dull boy. I believe it is unhealthy to spend all my time working. Work, study and leisure are all essential to our physical and mental health. If well- balanced, the three could bring about greater effects.

  1. What do you usually do during a break?
    I usually close my eyes to relax or l go out to breathe some fresh air. Sometimes, I will get myself a cup of tea or coffee.

Since school days are busy, the first thing I do during a break is stay at home, enjoy adequate sleep, and binge-watch the entire 10 seasons of Friends. After a full rest, I may fly to a foreign country like Korea with my besties and expose myself to a different culture.

  1. Do you prefer long break or several short breaks?
    I prefer one long break than several short breaks. Because several short breaks can easily distract people from staying focused on one thing. With long breaks, I can concentrate on my task to avoid making mistakes.

Well, it's a tough decision for me. I like them both, but if I have to choose between the two, I prefer a long break. During a long vacation like winter or summer vacation, I feel free to try various things like traveling abroad with family or friends, start a big project or finish an internship.

Number

  1. Are you good at math?
    I think I am quite good at math. Math is my favourite subject and I spend a lot time studying it. Among all of the aspects of Math, I am very skillful at finding the solutions to geometric problems.

I know that there is an Asian stereotype that Chinese students are all good at math. But to be honest, my favourite subject is English and I hate math, especially Calculus, since kindergarten. And it is because of math that I didn't choose science and technology as a major.

    I usually use numbers in my day to day life. Everything we do in our lives normally requires a little bit of math and numbers. So, mathematics plays an important role in the real world and I think it is the one of the most useful subjects.

Not really. As an English major, I haven't used numbers since college, for English is one of a few majors that does not require the students to learn advanced math. So numbers are Greek to me.

    I have to say I am not good at remembering phone numbers. I only remember the phone numbers of my parents and my boyfriend. This is because I usually use WeChat to video call my friends, so there is no need for me to remember their phone numbers.

Not at all. I am bad at remembering long sequence of numbers not logically arranged. As I aspire to become an excellent interpreter, this becomes one of my major weaknesses. Because a person with good short-term memory can keep in mind more information and messages within a limited period of time.

  1. What ‘s your favourite number?
    It must be 14. It was my student ID from my primary school to high school. It is very amazing that this was my number as my student for so many years, because the students IDs are usually given to students by the rank of their entrance exam scores.

My favourite number is 100. One of the reasons why is because it represents full marks and straight A academic excellence. Every time I get 100 points, I become more eager to learn new things. The other reason is it always reminds me of pure and perfect things without flaws. For example, if a beverage manufacturer uses the phrase 100% apple juice, it builds an honest and reliable image.

Park

  1. Do you prefer natural parks or amusement parks?
    I prefer natural parks. I love the feeling of peace and calm that I can find at a natural park. Though there are tons of fun things to do in an amusement park, I would be happier to take a walk in a forest or beside a lake.

  2. Do you think people like going to parks?
    There isn’t much open space in the city, all the buildings are close together and living spaces are tight. A park is a brilliant place to escape these things and it can help people unwind and relax.

Well, it was so, a decade ago. Because at that time, people preferred to chat with each other in person instead of on a smart phone. Therefore, parks served as an ideal place to interact with friends. These days, the trend is to hang out in a shopping mall or movie theatre.

  1. What activities can be done in a park?
    There are many activities that can be done in a park, such as playing badminton or football. But Chinese people especially like to do square dancing in the park. When the night falls, many retired people go to the parks, turn on the radio and start to square dance with the rhythm of music.

I believe a park is made for the purpose of family activities like flying kites with kids, going picnic, taking a walk with family members and walking a dog. And it becomes a trend for young couples to go on a date in the park. They stroll hand in hand, have sweet talks and smile to each other.

  1. Do you often go to a park?
    Not very often. However, whenever I feel anxious or something bad happens, I will go to the park to unwind. A park is a perfect place to escape the hustle and bustle of the city and the things that annoy me.

Not really often. With the rapid urbanisation in China, the city I live in is losing green spaces like trees, arks and lawns. As I grew up in a city full of skyscrapers, it is difficult for me to have fun in a park That's a real pity.
